Brand Overview

Mondelez International is a powerhouse in the world of confectionery, food, holding, beverage, and snack food, with a global presence spanning approximately 160 countries and annual revenues of around $26.5 billion. Since its inception in 2012, under the leadership of CEO Dirk Van de Put, the company has been committed to driving the future of snacking through sustainable and inclusive practices. Setting ambitious environmental goals, Mondelez International aims to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050 and ensure that 100% of its packaging is recyclable by 2025. The company is also dedicated to sustainable sourcing of key ingredients such as cocoa, wheat, and dairy, demonstrating its commitment to responsible business practices. Diversity and inclusion are at the core of Mondelez International's operations, with a focus on fostering a culture of equality and empowerment within the organization. The company places a strong emphasis on governance and ethics, ensuring that its business practices align with the highest standards of integrity. Looking ahead to 2023 and beyond, Mondelez International is focused on reducing greenhouse gas emissions, increasing renewable energy usage, and furthering sustainable sourcing efforts. The company is dedicated to enhancing transparency, setting new milestones for 2030, and integrating environmental, social, and governance (ESG) initiatives into its long-term strategy to create lasting value for all stakeholders. With a clear vision for the future, Mondelez International is on a mission to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050, increase female leadership representation, and expand its efforts in nutrition education and community volunteerism. By continually striving for excellence in sustainability, diversity, and community impact, Mondelez International is poised to lead the way in shaping the future of snacking for years to come.

Regenerative Agriculture Partnerships

Mondelez International's commitment to sustainable sourcing extends to regenerative agriculture partnerships. By collaborating with farmers and organizations, the company promotes practices that restore and enhance the health of the soil, increase biodiversity, and sequester carbon. These partnerships not only benefit the environment but also support the livelihoods of farmers and contribute to the resilience of agricultural systems. Through regenerative agriculture initiatives, Mondelez is working towards a more sustainable and regenerative food supply chain.

Female Leadership Representation

Mondelez International recognizes the importance of diversity and gender equality in leadership positions. The company is dedicated to increasing female representation in leadership roles across all levels of the organization. By fostering a culture of inclusivity and providing opportunities for women to advance in their careers, Mondelez aims to create a more diverse and equitable workforce. Through initiatives focused on mentorship, leadership development, and support networks, the company is committed to empowering women and breaking down barriers to gender equality in the workplace.
